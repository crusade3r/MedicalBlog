About Me:

My name is Nathan Henoke yilma, as of writing this (30/06/27), I am 17 years old completing my AS years at Bishopshalt School. I am an aspriring medical student who is interested in intrecate processes and the human body. My favourite food is pizza or chicken. My favourite colour is red.

In the future, I hope to become a cardiologist or a nephrologist in the NHS, but other pathways such as clinical physiology or Computing in Medicine are a viable and interesting option to me as well. I hope to be a doctor which inspires young patients, teaches mature patients and helps prolong one's life to the very best of my ability.

My passion for medicine came about in a year 10 lesson about homeostasis, specifically the kidneys. How such details is achieved everyday in your body astonished me (even though there were major GCSE simplifications). I was so inspired by the kidneys that I went home that very day and looked up jobs which research the organs of the human body. That led me to physiology. However that was simply not enough for me, and I realised that I enjoyed my life so much that I wanted to help others enjoy their life, so they could live on. During this revelation of mine, becoming a doctor made the most sense.
